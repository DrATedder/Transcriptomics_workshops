Project Overview

Purpose: Understand each of the steps in a transcriptomic workflow from dataset selection to functional interpretation using public gene expression data.

1. Introduction

This series of workshop activities introduces you to core bioinformatics tools and analytical reasoning required for effective gene expression data analysis. You will identify a relevant dataset, assess its experimental design, compute effect sizes, perform power and differential expression analysis, and interpret biological significance using functional annotation tools.

The aim is to develop both your technical fluency and your critical thinking about dataset suitability, statistical power, and biological relevance.

4. Things to Think About

  • Is the experimental design clear and biologically meaningful?
  • Do the sample sizes support robust inference? Consider power analysis.
  • Have treatments, time points, or confounders been well described?
  • Can effect sizes help you prioritise meaningful signals?
  • How will you explain or justify your filtering choices?
  • Are the results interpretable using current biological knowledge?
This project is as much about thinking critically as it is about using tools.

6. Final Outputs and Learning Goals

  • Annotated report of dataset evaluation and rationale for selection
  • Effect size summary and filtered gene lists
  • Power analysis results and plot
  • Differential expression table (GEO2R)
  • Functional enrichment results (DAVID)
  • Reflective commentary on strengths, weaknesses, and biological interpretation

7. Summary

By following this structured pipeline, you will learn how to handle real-world gene expression data and make justified analytical decisions. Keep notes, be critical, and discuss your reasoning.

Reminder: Ask questions, collaborate with peers, and don’t be afraid to explore!
