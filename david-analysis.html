Gene Ontology Analysis with DAVID

Goal: Use DAVID to interpret biological significance of differentially expressed genes (DEGs).

1. Introduction and Rationale

High-throughput experiments like RNA-Seq often yield long lists of DEGs. Interpreting these lists biologically is essential.

DAVID (Database for Annotation, Visualization and Integrated Discovery) helps determine whether certain biological functions or pathways are statistically enriched among your DEGs.

DAVID Website: https://david.ncifcrf.gov/

2. Concepts and Terminology

Gene Ontology (GO)
  • Biological Process (BP): e.g., cell division, apoptosis
  • Molecular Function (MF): e.g., kinase activity
  • Cellular Component (CC): e.g., nucleus, mitochondrion
KEGG Pathways

KEGG is a pathway database that maps genes to curated pathways like MAPK signaling or glycolysis.

Identifier Consistency

Ensure all gene identifiers are from the same system and organism. Mismatched or mixed identifiers lead to inaccurate results.

4. Step-by-Step Procedure

Step 1: Access DAVID

Go to https://david.ncifcrf.gov/ and click "Start Analysis".

Step 2: Upload Gene List
  • Select Gene List tab
  • Paste or upload your genes
  • Choose ID type (e.g., Official Gene Symbol)
  • Select species
  • Click Submit List
Step 3: Functional Annotation

Click Functional Annotation Tool to see enriched GO terms and KEGG pathways.

Step 4: Interpret Results

5. Statistical Considerations

  • Use Benjamini FDR < 0.05 to identify significant terms.
  • Filter out categories with < 2–3 gene hits for better robustness.
Fold enrichment shows how much more often a GO term appears in your gene list than by chance.

6. KEGG Pathway Analysis

DAVID maps your genes to known biological pathways. Click through KEGG maps to see highlighted genes in context.

KEGG Browser: https://www.genome.jp/kegg/pathway.html

7. Optional: Custom Background

For greater accuracy, upload a background gene list (e.g., all expressed genes in your dataset). This ensures the enrichment is calculated correctly.

8. Exporting Results

Use the export buttons in DAVID to download CSV or TSV files. You can then visualize the data using R (e.g., ggplot2) or clusterProfiler.
