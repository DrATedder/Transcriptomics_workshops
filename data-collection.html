Finding and Collecting Suitable Data

Goal: Identify, evaluate, and collect appropriate public gene expression data from the NCBI GEO repository for analysis.

1. Introduction

Before conducting any analysis, it is essential to find a dataset that aligns with your research question and meets basic quality and design criteria.

This guide walks you through how to explore datasets from the NCBI Gene Expression Omnibus (GEO), assess their experimental design, and collect supporting metadata and methodological details.

GEO Browser: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gds

2. Searching for a Dataset

Step 1: Use the GEO DataSets Portal
  • Go to https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/gds
  • Search using relevant keywords (e.g., “asthma treatment”, “avian influenza”, “liver fibrosis”)
  • Filter results by organism, study type (e.g., Expression profiling by array), or sample size
Step 2: Open a GEO Series (GSE) Record

Click on a GSE ID (e.g., GSE123456) to view detailed metadata, platform information, and sample description.

3. Assessing Suitability

Check the Experimental Design
  • Look for clear grouping: treatment vs. control, disease vs. healthy, etc.
  • Ensure at least 3 biological replicates per group
  • Beware of pseudoreplication or unclear group definitions
Evaluate Sample and Platform Information

Scroll to the "Sample table" or "Sample list" section. Pay attention to:

  • Sample annotations (e.g., group labels, treatment details)
  • Platform (GPL) used (e.g., Affymetrix Human Genome U133 Plus 2.0 Array)
  • Consistency of platform across samples
Download the Series Matrix File

Under “Download family”, look for the Series Matrix File(s). This file contains normalized expression data and sample annotations.

  • Right-click the link and choose "Save link as..."
  • Unzip if necessary (the app requires an uncompressed .txt matrix file)

4. Reviewing the Associated Publication

Step 1: Locate the Reference Paper
  • Scroll to the bottom of the GEO page for the “Relations” section
  • Click the PMID or DOI link to access the study publication
Step 2: Extract Methodological Details
  • Identify sample collection methods and inclusion/exclusion criteria
  • Understand treatment conditions, timepoints, or dosing if applicable
  • Note any batch effects or confounders discussed in the paper
Do not rely on GEO metadata alone. The associated paper often contains critical context for interpreting results.
